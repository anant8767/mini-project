Placement Process

Your journey from student to professional

1

Eligibility and Registration

Eligibility: Students become eligible for placement in their final year (usually the 8th semester).
Registration: Students must register with the Training & Placement (T&P) Cell to participate in the placement drives.

2

Preparation and Training

Skill Development: The college emphasizes skill-based learning and provides training to improve student employability.
Soft Skills: Students are encouraged to participate in soft skill training programs with 100% attendance, with the college planning to offer 60 hours of training.
Academic Record: Maintaining a good academic record is crucial, as most companies do not allow students with live backlogs to appear for placements.

3

Recruitment Process

Company Visits: The T&P cell invites various core and non-core companies to participate in the placement drives.
Recruitment Drives: Companies conduct their selection processes, which may include aptitude tests, technical interviews, and other rounds.
Final Selection: The final selection is based on a combination of the student's skills and their performance in the selection rounds.

4

Key aspects and tips for students

Start early: Begin building your skills and resume from your second year.
Focus on both academics and skills: Maintain a strong academic record while actively participating in skill-based training and activities.
Attend T&P cell activities: Regularly attend seminars and sessions organized by the T&P cell to stay informed and prepared.
Consider internships: Take advantage of internship opportunities, as they can sometimes lead to a Pre-Placement Offer (PPO).

Training Programs

Preparing you for success

Aptitude Training

Comprehensive training in quantitative aptitude, logical reasoning, verbal ability, and data interpretation. Regular tests to track progress and improvement.

Technical Training

In-depth sessions on data structures, algorithms, system design, database management, and programming languages. Hands-on coding practice and problem-solving.

Soft Skills Development

Communication skills, presentation techniques, email etiquette, and professional behavior training. Building confidence for interviews and workplace success.

Group Discussion Training

Practice sessions on various topics, learning to articulate thoughts clearly, listen actively, and lead discussions effectively in team settings.

Mock Interviews

Simulated interview experiences with industry professionals and faculty. Detailed feedback on technical knowledge, communication, and body language.

Resume Building

Workshops on creating impactful resumes and cover letters. One-on-one sessions to optimize your profile and highlight achievements effectively.
